Featured Projects

Radioship (Google News Initiative)

We tackled one of the most challenging problems in speech recognition: creating accurate systems for languages with limited resources. By combining cognitive understanding of language processing with advanced AI techniques, we developed an innovative pipeline that made audio content accessible in previously underserved languages.

Our innovative pipeline combined wav2vec-based speech recognition with sophisticated language modeling for output correction, achieving production-quality results for previously underserved languages.

The project demonstrates our ability to:

  • Design hybrid architectures that combine deep learning with classical NLP approaches
  • Optimize model performance under real-world constraints
  • Lead technical teams to deliver complex AI solutions on time and within budget

Complytron (Acquired by SEON)

As founder and Head of Data Science at Complytron, we developed an AI-powered compliance solution for the financial industry that was successful enough to be acquired by SEON. Our system combined advanced NLP techniques with domain-specific knowledge to automate complex compliance processes.

Key innovations:

  • Person and company entity recognition systems optimized for financial documents
  • Intelligent keyword detection that understood regulatory context
  • Scalable architecture processing millions of documents daily
  • Integration with existing financial workflows

Source Code Leak Data (Google Digital News Initiative)

Leading the data science team for this specialized search engine project, we created tools that helped investigative journalists navigate and analyze large-scale data leaks effectively. The system combined advanced web scraping capabilities with specialized search functionalities designed specifically for investigative reporting.

Our solution provided:

  • Intelligent document classification and clustering
  • Entity recognition optimized for investigative contexts
  • Secure and efficient search across massive document collections
  • Tools for identifying connections and patterns in complex datasets

Survey Analysis Innovation

In collaboration with Széchenyi University's Marketing and Management Department, we developed novel NLP methodologies for analyzing open-ended survey responses. Our approach combined probabilistic modeling with cognitive linguistics insights, revealing deeper patterns in qualitative data.

The project resulted in:

  • Published research on advanced survey analysis techniques
  • Scalable methodology for processing thousands of responses
  • Tools for extracting actionable insights from unstructured feedback
  • Framework for combining quantitative and qualitative analysis
